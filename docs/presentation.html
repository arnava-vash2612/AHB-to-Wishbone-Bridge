RTL Implementation & Verification

Submitted by: Arnava Vashishtha

Objective & Challenge

  • The Goal: Design an RTL bridge to enable seamless communication between a high-performance AHB Master and a Wishbone Slave.
  • The Core Challenge (Phase Mismatch):
    • AHB Protocol: Pipelined architecture. Address phase (Cycle 1) is decoupled from the Data phase (Cycle 2).
    • Wishbone Protocol: Flat architecture. Address, Control, and Data must be asserted concurrently in a single cycle.
  • The Solution: Implement a sequential buffer stage governed by an FSM to capture and delay AHB commands until the data phase aligns.

Architecture Block Diagram

The bridge maps pipelined AHB signals to flat Wishbone B.3 protocols in real-time.

Flow Control: The Stall Mechanism

The Necessity of Stalling: The AHB Master expects a rapid response, but target Wishbone Slaves frequently require wait states to process data.

Execution Strategy:

  • The bridge actively drives HREADYOUT = 0 during active Wishbone cycles.
  • This asserts backpressure, forcing the AHB Master to freeze its instruction pipeline.
  • Upon the Wishbone Slave asserting ACK_I, the bridge restores HREADYOUT = 1, finalizing the transfer and allowing the AHB Master to proceed.
